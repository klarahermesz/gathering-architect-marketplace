# Minimum Viable Community (MVC) Worksheet

A 9-question sprint to capture the DNA of a community before you invest in full design. Built on the Community Canvas MVC tool (community-canvas.org).

---

## How to use this worksheet

**Time:** 30–45 minutes
**Who:** 3–5 core stakeholders (founder + early collaborators or sponsors)
**Format:** Run it as a workshop. Silent writing first on each question, then surface answers together. Keep each answer to one or two sentences — the point is alignment, not perfection.
**Output:** A one-page community DNA summary. Use it to decide whether to invest in the full 17-theme Community Canvas, or to spot the answers you're least sure about (those are where deeper planning matters most).

### Before you start

Write the community's working name and the date at the top. If the name keeps changing during the session, that's a signal — note it in the gut-check at the end.

## The Nine Questions

Take roughly 3 minutes per question. Silent writing first, then surface as a group.

### 1. Purpose — Why does this community exist?

What void does it fill? What problem does it solve? If the community disappeared tomorrow, what would members miss?

### 2. Audience — Who is this for?

Be specific. "Marketing professionals" is too broad. "Mid-career in-house marketers at B2B SaaS companies who feel isolated in their function" is workable. Who is it *not* for is also worth naming.

> _Your answer:_

---

### 3. Values — What principles matter most?

Three to five values. What behaviors do you want to see? What would get someone gently pulled aside?

> _Your answer:_

---

### 4. Goals — What does success look like in 12 months?

Pick two or three measurable signals. Member count is a vanity metric; depth of relationship, frequency of contribution, and member-reported outcomes are stronger.

> _Your answer:_

---

### 5. Shared Experience — What happens regularly that brings members together?

The recurring thing. Could be a weekly call, a monthly dinner, a quarterly retreat, an annual conference, an always-on chat. If you can't name one, the community doesn't exist yet — name what you'd start with.

> _Your answer:_

---

### 6. Key Rituals — What traditions or repeated practices have meaning?

Different from shared experience: rituals are the small repeated acts that carry symbolic weight. Opening every gathering the same way. A welcome message for new members. A specific way members ask for help. Naming them matters because rituals are how culture gets transmitted.

> _Your answer:_

---

### 7. Organization — How are we organized? Who holds key roles?

Sponsor, community lead, core team, moderators, contributors. Doesn't have to be formal — just clear.

> _Your answer:_

---

### 8. Governance — How do we make decisions?

Who decides what? What requires the founder? What can the community decide? How are conflicts handled?

> _Your answer:_

---

### 9. Platforms — What tools or technology do we use?

Where do members find each other day-to-day? Where do gatherings happen? Where is knowledge stored?

## Synthesis and Gut-Check (5–10 minutes)

## Expanding to the full Community Canvas (17 themes)

Once you've validated with MVC, the full Canvas adds:

- Brand & Communication strategy
- Member Selection & Recruitment
- Member Journey & Transitions
- Content & Knowledge Sharing
- Roles & Participation (richer than the MVC "Organization" question)
- Rules & Norms (richer than the MVC "Values" question)
- Resources & Financing
- Data & Impact Measurement

See `references/community-frameworks.md` for the full 17-theme treatment.

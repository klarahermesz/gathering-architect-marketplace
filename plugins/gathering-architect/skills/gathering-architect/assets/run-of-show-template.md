# Run-of-Show Template

A time-by-time plan for a gathering, structured around the Kaospilot Learning Arch — SET, HOLD, LAND. Designed for sessions that are 90 minutes or longer. For shorter meetings, use the IDOARRT template instead.

> Energy management is the work. Time management is just the scaffold. A run-of-show is good when it shows you how the room will *feel* at each block, not only what will *happen*.

---

## How to use this template

**Time to fill in:** 30–60 minutes for a half-day; 60–90 minutes for a full day.
**When:** Any session 90 minutes or longer with more than one activity. Especially useful for workshops, off-sites, learning sessions, and any event with co-facilitators or a producer.
**Output:** A single page (or printable spread) that any co-facilitator could pick up and run from. The test: if you got food poisoning the night before, could your co-facilitator deliver this session from the run-of-show alone?

### Why the SET / HOLD / LAND arc

Most gatherings fail at the seams between activities, not inside them. The arc is a reminder that sessions need an opening that creates safety and purpose (SET), a productive middle that holds tension and challenge (HOLD), and a deliberate close that turns experience into commitment (LAND). Skipping LAND is the most common failure mode — facilitators run out of time and the session evaporates instead of landing.

## Pre-event checklist

The 80% of facilitation that happens before anyone walks in.

### T-7 days
- [ ] Invitations sent with clear purpose, time, and what to bring
- [ ] Pre-work (if any) distributed
- [ ] Materials list finalized
- [ ] Co-facilitator alignment call scheduled
- [ ] Room/platform confirmed
- [ ] Accessibility needs surveyed

### T-1 day
- [ ] Materials prepped and packed
- [ ] Slides/whiteboards/worksheets printed or loaded
- [ ] Co-facilitator dry-run completed
- [ ] Refreshments ordered (if in-person)
- [ ] Tech tested (if virtual or hybrid)
- [ ] Final attendee list reviewed for any context that might shift the design

### T-1 hour
- [ ] Room set up (chairs, tables, materials at each seat or station)
- [ ] Music ready for arrival energy
- [ ] Welcome sign / opening slide displayed
- [ ] Facilitators do a 5-minute centering before doors open

## The Run-of-Show

Each phase is a header. Each block under it gets a row in the table below. Fill in the blocks in order, then sanity-check by reading down the energy column — if you see three "high" rows in a row with no "reflective" between them, the design will exhaust the room.

## Post-event

Designed follow-through, not afterthought.

**Immediate (within 1 hour of close):**
- [ ] Photo of the whiteboard / artifacts captured
- [ ] Key decisions or commitments noted while fresh
- [ ] Facilitators do a 10-minute hot debrief (what worked, what to change)

**Within 48 hours:**
- [ ] Summary or recap sent to participants
- [ ] Action items routed to owners
- [ ] Co-facilitator debrief scheduled (longer, deeper than the hot debrief)

**Within 1 week:**
- [ ] Participant feedback collected (form, interviews, or async)
- [ ] Lessons captured for the next iteration of this session

## A worked example

A 3-hour learning workshop for 20 mid-career professionals new to working with AI tools.

**Purpose:** Give participants their first hands-on experience with a real AI workflow, and the confidence to try one new thing at work this week.

**Outcomes:**
1. Each participant ships one working prototype using an AI tool by the end of the session.
2. Each participant leaves with one named, scoped commitment for the week.
3. The room builds enough trust that participants will share what didn't work, not just what did.

**Energy arc:** social arrival → focused intro → high curiosity → productive struggle → relief and pride → reflective close

### SET (45 min, 25%)

| Time | Block | Activity | Purpose | Energy | Materials | Lead |
|------|-------|----------|---------|--------|-----------|------|
| 0:00–0:10 | Arrival | Music playing, name tags, coffee, casual greeting | Lower the entry barrier | Social | Name tags, music, coffee | Producer |
| 0:10–0:25 | Welcome + purpose | Lead names the why; quick room scan ("who's been doing this 6+ months / 6+ days / never") | Set frame, signal that all skill levels are welcome | Settling | Slide with one sentence on purpose | Lead |
| 0:25–0:45 | Impromptu Networking (3 rounds × 5 min) | Pairs rotate around the question "what's one thing AI has done for you that surprised you, or what's one thing you wish it could do for you?" | Lower stakes, generate energy, surface real questions | Building | Timer, bell | Lead |

### HOLD (120 min, 67%)

| Time | Block | Activity | Purpose | Energy | Materials | Lead |
|------|-------|----------|---------|--------|-----------|------|
| 0:45–1:00 | Demo | Lead shows one workflow end-to-end on screen | Set expectation for what "done" looks like | Focused | Screen, AI tool open | Lead |
| 1:00–1:15 | Guided practice | Whole group follows along, same prompt, same tool | Build first competence | Engaged | Worksheet with starter prompt | Co-facilitator |
| 1:15–1:20 | Quick break | Water, stretch, no tech | Reset before independent work | Reset | — | Producer |
| 1:20–2:20 | Independent build | Each participant works on their own use case; facilitators circulate | Real practice on real problems | Focused / productive struggle | Worksheet, AI tool, support from facilitators | Both facilitators |
| 2:20–2:45 | Show & Tell (pairs) | Pairs share what they built, what they learned, what didn't work | Surface learning across the room without spotlight pressure | Buzzy | Timer | Lead |

### LAND (35 min, 19%)

| Time | Block | Activity | Purpose | Energy | Materials | Lead |
|------|-------|----------|---------|--------|-----------|------|
| 2:45–2:55 | Whole-room highlights | A few volunteer pairs share one insight; lead synthesizes | Make the collective intelligence visible | Warm | Whiteboard | Lead |
| 2:55–3:05 | Commitment round | Each person writes one named, scoped commitment for the week on a card | Translate experience into next-Monday action | Reflective | Index cards, pens | Co-facilitator |
| 3:05–3:15 | Closing circle | Each person says one word for how they're leaving | Mark the close, capture mood | Settled | — | Lead |

**Risk register:**

| Risk | Likelihood | What we'll do |
|------|------------|---------------|
| Wi-Fi fails during independent build | Medium | Lead has hotspot; co-facilitator has offline backup prompts |
| One participant dominates pair shares | Medium | Lead names the time-boxing rule up front; co-facilitator interrupts gently if needed |
| Half the room is more advanced than expected | Medium-Low | Have a "stretch challenge" prompt ready for the independent build block |

## Final alignment check (do this with co-facilitators before doors open)

1. **Does the energy arc make sense?** Read down the energy column. Are there three high-energy blocks in a row with no reset? Three low-energy blocks with no spark?
2. **Will the LAND survive?** If the HOLD runs over by 10 minutes, what gets cut? Decide now, not in the moment.
3. **Who has the call?** If the room asks for something off-plan, who decides whether to adjust? Name them.
4. **What's the one thing we want to be different about how this lands than the last session like it?** Make it concrete.
